## Overview

PT Perkebunan Nusantara I needed an Interactive Asset Map Dashboard capable of visualizing the company's entire asset portfolio geospatially, providing comprehensive asset analytics, and integrating data from multiple internal and external sources. Alongside this, there was also a need to re-engineer the existing MAIA Mobile application to make it more modern and robust. This was a client project carried out during my internship and subsequent contract role at PT Greatsoft Solusi Indonesia.

## Key Features

- **Geospatial Visualization**: Displays all PTPN I assets on an interactive map using polygons, layers, and markers.
- **AI-Powered Smart Search**: An LLM-powered search box that allows users to query asset data using natural language.
- **Analytics and Reporting**: A dashboard combining charts and tabular views for analyzing asset status, value, and distribution.
- **Mobile Asset Management**: A re-engineered MAIA Mobile application for field asset identification, including offline mode support.
- **Multi-Source Integration**: Data integration with BMKG, BPBD, BPS, ESRI, SAP, Google Maps, and internal company systems.

## My Role and Contributions

I worked individually as the UI/UX Designer on this project, designing the entire system from end to end. This involved interpreting the client's business requirements independently, then translating them into the geospatial visualization, the analytics dashboard, and the MAIA Mobile re-engineering, all while accounting for the data integration requirements across BMKG, BPBD, ESRI, SAP, and Google Maps. The project included a complete design system, though no separate user flow document was produced.

## Challenges and Learnings

Working through a complex, multi-source system entirely on my own trained me to interpret business requirements independently and make design decisions without relying on team discussion, a skill that proved essential given the scope and technical complexity of the integrations involved.

## Outcome

The resulting Figma design system and prototype were used directly by the engineering team as their implementation reference, providing a clear and complete foundation for building out the geospatial asset dashboard and the re-engineered MAIA Mobile application.
